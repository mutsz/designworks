Overview




* A Global Network for Genomics.

Product

Their product is a cloud-based web platform for organizations that are pursuing genomic-based approaches to human health.

It allows bioinformaticians, biologists, scientists to analyze their massive volume of genomic data quickly and securely, and allow collaboration safely.

* Through discovery process.

Problem

DNAnexus platform failed to assist users to see the projects that need their attentions, thus they could efficiently make adjustment to their jobs (genomic data in the projects).

* Under the design process we met a tech issue. End up at a compromised design.

* Over 20 minor iterations, 7 major iteration, 2 are showcasing here.

Discovery

Why are we building a solution?

Through talking with scientists and observing their works, and categorizing hundreds of feedback from current users. We identify some obvious users' behaviors and notice that the current platform doesn't support with their regular behaviors.




Observation 1: Time is money.

Users keep close eyes on the progress and want to make sure nothing's unefficient and wrong since more time in platform means more money they need to pay.

Observation 2: Multitask environment.

All users work with 10-20 projects on average at once. And they participate as one of a collaboraters in each project.

Observation 3: Mostly, users log in only to check the status of their jobs

To ensure the project is working smoothly, they usually log in only to check the status of the jobs. They wish this process short and effortless.



Product Goal


"A more efficient way to evaluate the jobs, find risk and make actions."

Ideation

Then, we want to ask:

What do users want to see in our solution, so they could evaluate the project that need their attentions at a glance?

We further categorized the feedbacks into multiple "tasks". Then brought it to stakeholders and users to validate, add the questions that they might ask to solve the pain points mentioned in the quotes and define the "name" for the task which later become features.

* Other tasks that are also idealized: check Footprint, Check billing insight, monitor uploading data, View activities of teammates, quickly go to the recent/ favorite projects.

* We also try to prioritize the tasks, but we decide to let the user to tell us after the first wireframe idealized.

Knowing whether the design being applicable in real scenario.

Interviews 2

* Second interview.

Showing the interface as well as user's log of activities in the past week. Based on the log, we ask users to identify the ones that they regard most important and the reason for that.

And then we show the design and ask what could be improved.

More grouping and status: 1. User feel jobs in projects that billed to them, and jobs in project that launched by them should be prioritized first. 2. More status are needed for jobs.



Design decision


1. Visualization of jobs



Pros: Users are able to view all the jobs at once without the need to scroll, so they could capture all the information and stand in a higher level to evaluate projects (project).

Cons: The dots will overlap with one another so it becomes difficult to view one single job. Also, the visualization exphasizes the "launch time" of a job which is not as important as "duration". When user zooms in, the main identifier: information of duration is gone. Finally, it's comparably not easy to click a dot to view more details.


Pros: The information of duration is clearer, much intuitive.

Cons: It occupis more spaces and require user to scroll to see more data.

I selected bar over dot visualization. Based on the interview, duration is the main identifier for evaluation, and bars is a much intuitive visualization to represent the time. In order to make further action and decision, user need to view the details of a job, bars visualization could better accommodate job's information.


2. Layout of job statas filter and job visualization.



Pros: It leaves more spaces for the visualization. Overall, layout of the page is clearer. Also, it follows the interaction model designed in the Style Guide.

Cons: The height of the feature becomes longer in order to accommodate more vertical sectors of information.


Pros: Vertically, it becomes less crowded.

Cons: Page is devided into three sectors; as a result, it's much inorganized.

I decided to place the job status filter on top in order to follow the interaction model defined in the Style Guide to ensure users always stay oriented across the pages in the system.

3. Details of job



Pros: It could include more details at once.

Cons: Users need to click to view details of job. It occupies too much space so lesser jobs could be displayed. Difficult to compare between jobs.


Pros: User could identify which job is going wrong immediately and start to make actions. Could accommodate more job data.

Cons: Lesser details could be displayed.

I decided to selectively show important details of jobs, and more jobs in the visualization to help user evaluating and make actions.

Interaction design


Design for long loading

Considering the response time between action and response could be over 1s (which will give user a sense of interruption), I defined the interactions for loading the information when users log in the dashboard.

Lead users to understand the hierachical information displayed

From project scope on the left to amount of job status on top to data visualization view, the interaction is stratigically designed to display these three chunks of information gradually to teach users their hierachical relationships.



a - Expanding more project in side navigation

b - Allowing users to access to project

Project scope navigation

a - Expand to see more

Considering the scalability of the interface, more projects are hidden until user intends to see more. The smooth animation redure the "change blind" effect and inform users of the extra elements.

b - Only display access to project icon when hovering

While the side navigation mainly assist user to switch between project scope they are interested in, the feature accessing to project page is first hidden for minimalist and aesthetic design, and avoid distraction.

Reflection



From concept to concrete.

Allowing myself to test, create and experiment with alternative ideas is vital for a thoughtful final design. And starts from a very rough design presentation is a key skill to facilitate possibilities. As a UX designer, I want to always remind myself to embrace the art of "ugly sketches".

Design with users, but plan ahead.

Planning ahead of what's the question to be answered from our users is vital. Am I looking for feedback on product direction, feature defining or interface / interaction design? While the line between ideation and finalizing could be blurred within the process, defining a goal before engaging with my users is extremely helpful.

Let the product goal be the guide.

Tech issues, feasibility concerns and contradicted stakeholders' opinions were never ended during the product design process. Having a clear product goal allows me to rethink our product problems and avoid designing features that could distract team efforts.
