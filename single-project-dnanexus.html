Creating a visual language from the start is Exciting but Challenging.



For three years, I designed websites and Company identity systems for my clients. Improvements are pushed forward from continuous iterations, which mostly based on my intuition and feedbacks from user research.

→


This time... I built a style guide for DNAnexus. Through out the process, I transparentized, deconstructed and systemized my inner visualizing process. My ultimate goal is competing a repetitive usable library which enforces consistency and efficient communication.



Why building?

User interfaces are Inconsistant across DNAnexus system's pages.

By building a style guide, I am a step forward to transparentize, deconstruct and systemize my inner visualizing process, and make it a repetitive usable library which enforces consistency and efficient communication in DNAnexus.

For Developers...it saves time.

Working in an Agile based team, engineers found it difficut to communicate along the way refining and adding the features.

And obviously, as product grows larger in scale, with expansion of the features and pages, incosistent user experience become a concern. A systematic management is needed. I was assigned to build one for DNAnexus in 2016, May.


(Previous DNAnexus sign in and sign out page)

How?

To capture the conceptual ideas, I began with a Styleguide structure. To illustrate this structure, please think of it as a ball. From the inner to outer layer of the ball, four components are included: 1. Fundemental elements, 2. UI Components, 3. Templates, 4. What users see.
