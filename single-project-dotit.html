We heard from our female friends' and sisters' pregnancy experience.

Imagine your first time going to school. It's a mix of excitement but concerns.

We feel the same way of our loved ones who's experienceing their first pregnancy. While filled with joy, they also face all the uncertainties they'd ever faced. Symptoms of nausea and vomiting is their very first, and, constantly been reported, the most annoying and unpredictable one.


It is also an interesting timing that they encounter this symptom. They don't tend to talk much about this fantastic news in the beginning. And while they almost look no different than before, they keep their routine clock and go work. Knowing them experiencing such unstable period, we designed Dot-it.











Understand the NVP symptoms

In order to get a more comprehensive understandings of the NVP symptom, we conducted a series of 9 in-depth interviews and selectively reviewed 20 research articles. In each interview, the participant was asked for his or her experience and coping mechanism for NVP.

And, we got some exciting findings.

Their NVP symtoms could occur at particular times of day, but the patterns could not be noticed at the very beginning of their pregnancy. Triggers of NVP include unpleasant smells, food, teeth brushing and over-eating.

Some Essential characteristics of NVP:

Their NVP symptoms continued until completely disappeared after four months of pregnancy. It has predictable daily patterns as well as unpredictable triggers.

Also, non-pharmaceutical approaches to relieve NVP were preferred and predominantly used.

Again, they all mentioned that the uncomfortness of NVP has a significant impact both on their productivity in the work space as well as the overall quality of life.





Before design, we have a couple of design requirement in mind.

1. Ease of use in recording NVP.

Because pregnant women felt tired most of the time, complicate and frequent recording would further exhaust them.

2. Reduction of time using the system.

According to interview, long-term use of screen devices will trigger feelings of nausea and vomiting.

3. Lack of interfaces that would become associated with feelings of nausea and vomiting

Avoid representations of foods and odors which had triggered nausea and vomiting in the past.

Features to help releasing the NVP symtoms

We use a storyborad to help us visualizing the features that may help solving the problems.

1. Record NVP

Recording when users are experiencing the symptoms.

2. Explore NVP history

Exploring user's symptoms history.

3. Actively Schedule to avoid NVP

Based on the history, actively making schedule to avoid discomfort.

User test & Design decision

We asked 8 pregnant women to test the prototype. We also asked 6 partners to test the notification function and interviewed them about their feedback.

"I can see myself recording and exploring the NVP pattern with your design..but how could I record while I am having the symptoms?"

Users liked the recording and the exploring NVP pattern functions. We need to come up with a easy way to record when they are experiencing uncomfort.



[Before] Users need to open the app to record.


[After] When users come into a feeling of nausea, she should press the button of the acupressure band to record and release pain.

"Vomiting always comes with Nausea, so it is hard for me to choose one when recording."



Users wanted more focus on the degree of discomfort rather than the type (nausea, vomiting). As a result, instead of letting users to record whether they are having nausea and vomiting, we let them indicate the degree of uncomfortness. Which is also much useful for them to evaluate. We also let the user to confirm additional information when they feel better.

Originally, the users will need to indicate Nausea or Vomiting that they were experiencing.



"It's hard to see the pattern with texts and numbers."

Users wanted to see graphic visualization of history data beyond the text and table we provided. And they want to see the patterns over time.

Text view of the records.

Visualization view of the records (in terms of week).

Following our design requirements, we have designed Dot-it system to assist pregnant women in managing NVP.

An acupressure wristband for recording NVP episodes.

& A mobile app for exploring NVP patterns and managing life with NVP during pregnancy.

Recording
the symptoms.

Push and hold the button of the acupressure band to record the event. Data of the event will be sent to the mobile device for user to confirm.

Exploring
the patterns.

Visualized the data history to help users explore their NVP pattern.

Scheduling
life and work.

Showing the possible occurrence of incoming episode based on history.
